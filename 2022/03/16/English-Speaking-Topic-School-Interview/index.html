    English Speaking Topic: Graduate School Interview Questions
    2022-03-16|English
    字数总计: 630|阅读时长: 3 分钟

    Here are some questions commonly asked in graduate school interview in China.

    1. Tell me a little about yourself.
    2. Please describe your character.
    3. What are your strengths?
    4. What are your weakness?
    5. What’s your favorite book?
    6. Please introduce your university.
    7. Why did you choose our university?
    8. Why did you choose your major?
    9. Do you like your major? Why?
    10. Which course do you like best?
    11. Why do you choose to further your study instead of going to work?
    12. What are your research interests?
    13. What is your plan in the postgraduate study?
    14. Could you tell me something about your family?
    15. Tell me something about your hometown.
    16. Can you tell me a little about the city where your undergraduate school is located?
    17. What are your spare time interests?

    Q1: Tell me a little about yourself.

    My name is XXX, and I’m XXX years old. I attended Nanjing University of Posts and Telecommunications where I majored in information security. My interest focuses on information security, especially cryptology, and I’d like to further my study at your university.

    You can call me a hard worker. During the four-year undergraduate study. I have not only mastered some practical skills about computer programming, but I have also built a solid theoretic foundation. In the second year I learned cryptography at the university, and I found myself have a passion for it. Afterwards, when I became a junior, I revised number theory and abstract algebra. This allowed to join a CTF team and made me an opportunity to contact more cryptology in CTF challenges. In the senior year, I selected lattice basis reduction as my thesis topic.

    Q2: Please describe your character.

    Maybe I’m not very easy-going, but I think my friend would describe me as warm-hearted, friendly and responsible. In addition, I’m a hard worker and I like to challenge myself to improve at everything I do.

    Q3: What are your strengths?

    I’m curious, patient and very honest. I always try to find out the essence of a subject itself. Not only I want to remember the knowledge, but I want to clearly understand it as well. What’s more, I always do things in a right and honest way. You can fool other people, but you can’t fool yourself. That’s what I believe.

    Q4: What are your weakness?

    1. This might be bad, but in college I found that I procrastinated sometimes. I realized this problem, and I’m working on it by finishing my work ahead of schedule.
    2. Sometimes I’m too detail oriented. I don’t want to leave anything out and I want be perfect. This is bad because it slows down my work. To improve my efficiency, I’m trying to put more emphasis on priority and planning. I hope this can help me to find a balance between quantity and quality.

    Q7: Please introduce your university.

    Q13: What is your plan in the postgraduate study?

    Q14: Could you tell me something about your family?

    Q: Tell me something about your hometown.

    I’m from Tianjin, a big city on the east coast of China. It’s located to the south of Beijing, only 100 kilometers away. It has a history of 600 years since its establishment in the 14th century. It relies mainly on commerce and service industry. Tianjin is famous for its food and entertainment, and it enjoys a reputation of a “motherland of quyi”. It’s a really beautiful and modern big city.

    Q16: Can you tell me a little about the city where your undergraduate school is located?

    Well, my university is located in Nanjing. Nanjing is a big city in the southeast of China…
